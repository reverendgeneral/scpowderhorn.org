Introduction and Learning Objectives

Powder Horn is a training opportunity designed to expose Venturing or Boy Scout leaders and youth members to activities and resources necessary to operate a successful high adventure program in several ways:

  • Help Leaders to safely conduct outdoor activities of a fun and challenging nature
  • Provide an introduction to the resources necessary to successfully lead members through a program of high adventure
  • Allow youth members to experience high adventure activities in a safe non-threatening environment

After completing this course, participants should have introductory information that will provide the following outcomes:

  • Knowledge of the health and safety requirements, rules, and regulations of the BSA as they apply to outdoor programs
  • Introduction to the skills necessary to operate an outdoor program
  • Knowledge of how to identify local resources and consultants for high adventure activities
  • Promote youth and adult creativity when delivering high adventure programs

Participant Qualifications

In order to participate in the Sequoyah Council Powder Horn, applicants must:

  • Be a registered member of BSA
  • Complete required training for their leader position, VEnture YPT, and Hazardous Weather before the course
  • Be able to meet physical requirements of BSA Class III Health form
  • Youth applicants must be approved by their unit leader and the course director and must be 14 years old
